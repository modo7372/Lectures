Ulcerative Colitis
Overview
Ulcerative colitis (UC) is part of Inflammatory Bowel Diseases (IBDs)
IBDs are:
Chronic Self-perpetuating inflammatory disorders Of unknown etiology
Affect the gastrointestinal (GI) tract
Epidemiology
Bimodal age distribution:
Peak 1: 15–30 years
Peak 2: 60–80 years
Can occur at any age
Pathogenesis
Immune activation due to unknown environmental trigger
Occurs in genetically predisposed individuals
Possible triggers:
Microorganisms
Dietary components
Bacterial antigens
Self-antigens
➡️ Leads to immune dysregulation
Genetic evidence:
Increased risk in first-degree relatives
Increased risk in monozygotic twins
Genetic Associations
Ulcerative colitis: 
HLA-DR2
Crohn’s disease:
CARD15 gene (Chromosome 16p)
Accounts for ~10% of cases

Immunological 
Markers
p-ANCA:
Present in ~70% of UC 
patients
Also in 5–10% of CD 
patients
ASCA:
Present in 60–70% of CD patients
Found in: 10–15% of UC patients
~5% of normal individuals
Disease Course Characterized by:
Remissions and exacerbations
Triggers of flares:
Infections
NSAIDs
Stress
Impact on Patients ➡️ Significantly affects quality of life
Management Challenges
Despite:
Immunosuppressants
Biologic therapies
➡️ Some cases remain difficult to manage
