Ulcerative colitis
Inflammatory bowel diseases (IBDs) are chronic self-perpetuating inflammatory disorders of unknown etiology involving the gastrointestinal (GI) tract. It has bimodal age of distribution, Peak occurrence is between ages 15 and 30 and between ages 60 and 80, but onset may occur at any age

---

UC
Pathogenesis of IBD involves activation of immune cells by unknown environmental trigger in genetically predisposed individual (? microorganism, dietary component, bacterial or self-antigen) leading to immune dysregulation. Genetic component suggested by increased risk of IBD in the first-degree relatives of patients and in monozygotic twins

---

UC
Reported genetic associations include HLA-DR2 in Ulcerative Colitis(UC) patients and a Crohn’s disease (CD)-related gene (CARD15) on chromosome 16p. CARD15 mutations may account for 10% of CD risk.

---

UC
Other potential pathogenic factors include serum antineutrophil cytoplasmic antibodies (ANCA) in 70% of pts with UC (also in 5–10% of CD pts) and antibodies to Saccharomyces cerevisiae (ASCA) in 60–70% of CD pts (also in 10–15% of UC pts and 5% of normal controls).

---

UC
This disease entity almost follows the course of remissions and exacerbations. Acute flares may be precipitated by infections, nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), and stress
