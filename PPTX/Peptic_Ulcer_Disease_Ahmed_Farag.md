## Slide 2
Introduction
• Peptic ulcer disease (PUD) refers to mucosal erosions in the stomach or duodenum
• Results from imbalance between mucosal defense and acid-peptic injury
• Insert diagram: Stomach and duodenum anatomy schematic

---

## Slide 3
Epidemiology
• Common gastrointestinal disorder worldwide
• Duodenal ulcers more common than gastric ulcers
• Peak incidence: 30–60 years
• Insert diagram: Global prevalence chart (blue schematic)

---

## Slide 4
Etiology
• Helicobacter pylori infection
• NSAID use
• Zollinger-Ellison syndrome (rare)
• Stress, smoking, alcohol
• Insert diagram: Etiologic factors flowchart

---

## Slide 5
Pathophysiology
• Increased acid and pepsin secretion
• Impaired mucosal barrier defense
• Role of H. pylori and NSAIDs in mucosal injury
• Insert diagram: Mechanism of ulcer formation schematic

---

## Slide 6
H. pylori Infection
• Gram-negative spiral bacterium colonizing gastric mucosa
• Produces urease, neutralizing gastric acid
• Causes chronic gastritis and ulceration
• Insert diagram: H. pylori colonization process schematic

---

## Slide 7
NSAID-Induced Ulcers
• Inhibition of COX-1 → decreased prostaglandins
• Reduced mucus and bicarbonate secretion
• Increased mucosal susceptibility to acid
• Insert diagram: NSAID effect on gastric mucosa

---

## Slide 8
Clinical Features
• Epigastric pain (burning, gnawing)
• Pain related to meals (duodenal vs gastric)
• Nausea, bloating, belching
• Insert diagram: Pain timing differences schematic

---

## Slide 9
Complications
• Bleeding (hematemesis, melena)
• Perforation → peritonitis
• Gastric outlet obstruction
• Penetration into adjacent organs
• Insert diagram: Complications flowchart (blue arrows)

---

## Slide 10
Diagnosis
• Upper GI endoscopy is the gold standard
• H. pylori detection: biopsy urease test, stool antigen, urea breath test
• Insert diagram: Diagnostic algorithm schematic

---

## Slide 11
Endoscopic Findings
• Duodenal ulcer: anterior/posterior bulb
• Gastric ulcer: lesser curvature
• Insert diagram: Endoscopic appearance schematic

---

## Slide 12
Laboratory Tests
• CBC for anemia
• Stool occult blood
• H. pylori testing
• Insert diagram: Lab test summary table

---

## Slide 13
Medical Management
• Eradicate H. pylori: triple or quadruple therapy
• Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs)
• Stop NSAIDs if possible
• Lifestyle modifications
• Insert diagram: Treatment protocol schematic

---

## Slide 14
Surgical Management
• Indications: bleeding, perforation, obstruction, non-healing ulcer
• Procedures: vagotomy, pyloroplasty, partial gastrectomy
• Insert diagram: Surgical options schematic

---

## Slide 15
Complications of Surgery
• Dumping syndrome
• Bile reflux gastritis
• Anastomotic ulcer
• Nutritional deficiencies
• Insert diagram: Post-surgical complication flowchart

---

## Slide 16
Follow-Up and Prognosis
• Repeat endoscopy for gastric ulcers
• Excellent prognosis with H. pylori eradication
• Risk of recurrence if infection persists
• Insert diagram: Follow-up pathway schematic

---

## Slide 17
Prevention
• Avoid NSAIDs or use protective agents
• Eradicate H. pylori in high-risk individuals
• Stop smoking, limit alcohol
• Insert diagram: Preventive measures chart

---

## Slide 18
Take-Home Messages
• PUD is a common but treatable cause of upper GI pain
• H. pylori and NSAIDs are major culprits
• PPIs and eradication therapy form the cornerstone of treatment
• Complications require urgent attention
• Insert diagram: Summary mind map (blue schematic)
